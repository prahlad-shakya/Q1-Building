Building Codes and Regulations

Building codes are legal requirements that dictate the standards for construction, design, and maintenance of structures. Compliance ensures buildings are safe, accessible, and sustainable.

  • National and local building codes
  • Zoning and land-use regulations
  • Fire safety and egress standards
  • Accessibility
  • Structural integrity and seismic codes

Mechanical, Electrical, and Plumbing Compliance

Engineering compliance covers the MEP systems to ensure they are designed, installed, and maintained according to regulations and best practices.

  • Electrical load capacity and wiring standards
  • HVAC system efficiency and ventilation codes
  • Plumbing standards and water quality regulations
  • Energy conservation and sustainability

Health, Safety, and Environmental Compliance

Buildings must adhere to standards that protect the health and safety of occupants and minimize environmental impact. This includes proper waste disposal, air quality, and emergency systems.

  • OSHA standards for workplace safety
  • Indoor air quality and ventilation
  • Fire detection and suppression systems
  • Emergency exits, alarms, and signage
  • Hazardous material handling and disposal

Inspection, Documentation, and Certification

Regular inspections and proper documentation are essential to prove compliance. Certifications provide formal recognition that the building meets all applicable standards and regulations.

  • Scheduled inspections
  • Maintaining compliance logs and service records
  • Third-party audits and assessments
  • Certification processes
